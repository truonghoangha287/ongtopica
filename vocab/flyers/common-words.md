# Flyers — Common Words and Phrases (new at this level)

## Adverbs
| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| actually | Actually, I prefer tea. |
| almost | She almost wins the race. |
| already | Have you already eaten? |
| apparently | Apparently, the film is very good. |
| approximately | It takes approximately two hours. |
| at least | Drink at least two litres of water a day. |
| automatically | The door opens automatically. |
| barely | She barely passes the exam. |
| completely | He is completely right. |
| constantly | She constantly checks her phone. |
| definitely | I'll definitely be there. |
| especially | She loves animals, especially cats. |
| eventually | She eventually finds the answer. |
| exactly | The meeting is at exactly nine o'clock. |
| extremely | The weather is extremely hot. |
| fortunately | Fortunately, no one was hurt. |
| generally | She generally gets up at seven. |
| gradually | He gradually gets better. |
| hardly | She hardly ever eats fast food. |
| immediately | Call the doctor immediately! |
| increasingly | Air pollution is increasingly bad. |
| mainly | They mainly eat fish. |
| mostly | She mostly reads novels. |
| nearly | She is nearly twelve years old. |
| obviously | Obviously, you need to study. |
| particularly | She is particularly good at science. |
| personally | Personally, I prefer pizza. |
| possibly | Could you possibly help me? |
| previously | She previously lived in France. |
| recently | She recently started a new job. |
| significantly | Temperatures have risen significantly. |
| slightly | She feels slightly better. |
| specifically | She specifically asks for vegetarian food. |
| strongly | I strongly recommend this book. |
| successfully | She successfully completes the challenge. |
| surprisingly | Surprisingly, she wins. |
| typically | Typically, the lesson lasts an hour. |
| unfortunately | Unfortunately, the match is cancelled. |

## Prepositions and Phrases
| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| according to | According to the report, it will rain. |
| apart from | Apart from maths, she loves all subjects. |
| as a result | She trained hard; as a result, she won. |
| as well as | She speaks French as well as English. |
| at the moment | At the moment, she is studying. |
| by the time | By the time we arrived, it was too late. |
| due to | The match is cancelled due to rain. |
| for example | For example, lions eat meat. |
| however | However, the plan changes. |
| in addition | In addition, she plays the piano. |
| in fact | In fact, the problem is easy. |
| in order to | She studies hard in order to pass. |
| in spite of | In spite of the rain, they play outside. |
| instead of | Use this instead of that. |
| on the other hand | On the other hand, it could be worse. |
| such as | Animals such as lions eat meat. |
| therefore | She studied hard; therefore she passed. |

## Discourse Markers
| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| although | Although it was cold, they went outside. |
| as a result | She trained hard, as a result she won. |
| consequently | He was late; consequently, he missed the bus. |
| despite | Despite the rain, she cycles to school. |
| furthermore | Furthermore, she is very talented. |
| in conclusion | In conclusion, the project was successful. |
| moreover | Moreover, she is great at teamwork. |
| nevertheless | Nevertheless, she keeps trying. |
| on the contrary | On the contrary, the food was delicious. |
| on the whole | On the whole, the event was a success. |
| to sum up | To sum up, the project went well. |
| whereas | She likes hot weather, whereas he prefers cold. |
